<interfaces>
`ZeroScopeClearanceProof` currently has only canonical reconciliation facts and freshness timestamps. `KillSwitchService` independently calls the collector for begin and complete, but direct ledger callers can replay the same proof. `_zero_scope_proof_matches_current_state_in_transaction()` validates canonical round-trip and the 60-second window only.

Keep `ZeroScopeClearanceProof` ID-free and non-submittable. Add an optional, opaque transition challenge field that is absent for begin and mandatory for complete; it is not an assessment ID, ticket, command, claim, permit, outbound value, or gateway capability. On a successful zero-scope begin, SQLite creates a cryptographically random challenge and a pending transition row containing the begin event identity, canonical begin-proof digest, durable transition start time, durable `challenge_expires_at_utc` computed with the fixed 60-second transition TTL, and status. On complete, SQLite must require the exact pending challenge, a canonical proof digest different from the begin digest, a proof collection timestamp strictly after the durable begin transition time, and a current transaction time strictly before `challenge_expires_at_utc`. It must perform that expiry check in the same `BEGIN IMMEDIATE` before the conditional pending-to-consumed update (`rowcount == 1`) and before any READY audit/state write. A direct replay of the begin proof cannot satisfy these checks, and a forged/random/reused/expired challenge cannot be accepted even when the supplied complete proof and all reconciliation facts are otherwise current. The existing canonical/fresh fact checks and `_recovery_facts_are_clear_in_transaction()` remain mandatory for both transitions. Nonempty recovery scopes must continue to reject a zero-scope proof and use their unchanged exact current assessment-ID validation.
</interfaces>
